Wake Log

Fits a fully conjugate Bayesian linear model — joint posterior over coefficients and noise variance, empirical-Bayes shrinkage on day-of-week effects, and a Bayes factor test for whether day-of-week is even justified. Also checks whether wake time should be modeled as circular (von Mises) rather than linear.

Generative model
yi = xiTθ + εi,  εi ~ N(0, σ²)  |  θ | σ² ~ N(m₀, σ²V₀)  |  σ² ~ InvGamma(a₀, b₀)
Joint conjugate posterior (Normal–Inverse-Gamma, exact closed form):
Vn = (V₀⁻¹ + XTX)⁻¹  ·  mn = Vn(V₀⁻¹m₀ + XTy)  ·  an = a₀ + n/2
bn = b₀ + ½(m₀TV₀⁻¹m₀ + yTy − mnTVn⁻¹mn)  →  θ|y ~ Student-t(mn, (bn/an)Vn, df=2an)

3 Is day-of-week justified? Bayes factor

Comparing the full model (with day-of-week effects) against a reduced model (without) via marginal likelihood. A Bayes factor near 1 means day-of-week isn't earning its added complexity — Occam's razor, made quantitative.

4 Is wake time even linear? von Mises check

Clock time wraps at midnight — a Gaussian on minutes-past-midnight silently breaks if your wake times cluster near that boundary. This checks circular concentration (R, κ) and reports a circular mean for comparison.
